Hi, @channel! I have condensed and organized the MINAS fields based on our meeting last week. You will find the edits in the first three tabs:

‚Ä¢ MIxS Core-pathogens/microbes - fields from MIxS core that we decided are essential for pathogen/microbe aDNA ‚Ä¢ EnvPackages-pathogens/microbes - fields from EnvPackages that we decided were applicable for pathogen/microbe aDNA ‚Ä¢ MINAS extension-pathogens/microbes - fields that we decided were important for pathogen/microbe aDNA that are not present in MIxS Core or EnvPackages I copied James‚Äôs formatting to make things easier to collate down the road.

Overall, our biggest concerns/points of discussion for the next meeting are

  1. How do we deal with taxon/organism-specific fields when the raw data from one library may contain >1 studied microbe from the publication and will contain many unstudied microbes not reported/analyzed in the publication?
  2. Is it necessary to repeat content between base ENA/SRA upload, MIxS core, and EnvPackage fields? For example, can we just include the project name in the base ENA metadata entry, or do we have to repeat it for each package?
  3. Do we want to include metadata that is independent of the sample and the uploaded raw data? For example, do we want to include fields for screening and decontamination software and parameters? Please have a look and feel free to change anything, but please leave a comment so we can follow the changes until our next discussion. I drafted some options for burial-context. I am not married to the proposed options, but I do think it needs to be required and not optional so people are more likely to provide the information.
